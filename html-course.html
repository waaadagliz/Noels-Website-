Lesson 8: Media in HTML (Images, Audio, Video)

HTML allows you to add images, music, and videos to your websites. Here's how to do it:

<!-- Image Example -->
<img src="https://via.placeholder.com/150" alt="Placeholder Image">

<!-- Audio Example -->
<audio controls>
  <source src="https://www.soundhelix.com/examples/mp3/SoundHelix-Song-1.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

<!-- Video Example -->
<video width="320" height="240" controls>
  <source src="https://www.w3schools.com/html/mov_bbb.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

Lesson 10: Links and Navigation in HTML

Links are used to connect web pages. You can link to other websites or your own pages using the <a> tag.

<!-- External Link -->
<a href="https://www.google.com" target="_blank">Visit Google</a>

<br><br>

<!-- Internal Link -->
<a href="about.html">Go to About Page</a>

<br><br>

<!-- Navigation Bar Example -->
<nav>
  <a href="index.html">Home</a> |
  <a href="courses.html">Courses</a> |
  <a href="contact.html">Contact</a>
</nav>

Output:

Lesson 11: HTML Entities & Special Characters

Sometimes we need to show characters like < or © in our HTML. Here's how:

<p>&lt;h1&gt;This is a heading&lt;/h1&gt;</p>
<p>Copyright &copy; 2025 Noel Codes</p>
<p>Registered &reg;</p>
<p>Use &amp; to display an ampersand (&)</p>
<p>Euro: &euro;</p>
<p>Non-breaking space: Hello&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;World</p>

Lesson 12: Embedding Content in HTML

You can embed videos, maps, and even full webpages using the <iframe> tag.

<!-- Embed YouTube Video -->
<iframe width="560" height="315" 
src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/dQw4w9WgXcQ" 
title="YouTube video" frameborder="0" 
allowfullscreen></iframe>

<!-- Embed Google Map -->
<iframe 
src="https://www.google.com/maps/embed?pb=!1m18..." 
width="560" height="315" style="border:0;" 
allowfullscreen loading="lazy"></iframe>
